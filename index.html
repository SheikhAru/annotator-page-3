Your task is to identify metaphor from the given texts. Before that, let’s see the definition and few examples of metaphor.

Definition: A metaphor is a figure of speech that, for rhetorical effect, refers to one thing by mentioning another.

Example 1: “They've already stood the test of time and will stay with us for a long time.”

Explanation: The phrase “stood the test of time” metaphorically refers to lasting reliability or endurance by comparing it to passing a difficult trial.

Example 2: “and living in a house decorated like it would have been 200 years ago would be a great way to feed my imagination even more!”

Explanation: The metaphor compares stimulating imagination to feeding it like food, suggesting that the old-fashioned house would nourish creativity.

Example 3: “Going to job interviews as an adult and having a childish person talk to your acne the whole time.”

Explanation: The metaphor compares the experience of job interviews to being treated like a child, by describing the interviewer’s focus on acne instead of the applicant’s professionalism.
